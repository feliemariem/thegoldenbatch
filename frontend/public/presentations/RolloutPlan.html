Website Rollout Plan
2-Phase Approach
Committee Reference · February 2026

We need to show movement.

This document outlines the 2-phase rollout plan for thegoldenbatch2003.com. Phase 1 gets everyone in the door. Phase 2 builds everything behind the scenes before presenting the contribution framework through proper channels.

Phase 1 · Must
Launch Registration
March 1, 2026

Get everyone in the door. No money talk yet.

What goes live
Registration only. Batchmates receive their invite, create an account, and confirm their RSVP.
What they see
A welcome page with a "stay tuned for more" message. Clean, simple, no contribution or tier information visible.
What's NOT live
The new profile page (contribution tiers, payment uploads, progress tracking) stays admin-only until Phase 2 is ready.
The goal
Build the registered base. See who's engaged, which sections are responsive. This data informs Phase 2 decisions.
Overseas alumni
~30% of the batch are abroad. December 2028 means flights and leave planning. Getting them registered early is critical.
Committee goes first
The first ones to register will be the committee members. This ensures the system works smoothly before the batch comes in. Any new features coded after launch will be tested by the committee first (c/o Felie) before going live to everyone.
Phase 2 · Behind the Scenes
Contribution Rollout
While registrations come in

While Phase 1 registrations are coming in, the committee works on everything below. Nothing goes public until all steps are complete and the core has approved.

Step 1 Finalize Tier Presentation Internally

Align the committee on exact messaging and the "why" behind the numbers. Review the contribution tiers (Platinum, Gold, Silver, Flex), monthly breakdown, and the 80/20 funding allocation model. This is the foundation — everything downstream depends on the committee being aligned here first.

Step 2 Draft the FAQ

Anticipate the questions before they come. Cover: tier flexibility (can I change?), payment methods, where the money goes, "what if I can't afford it," installment options, the timeline, and what happens to their payments if things change. The FAQ should be ready to hand off to class reps as their primary reference.

Step 3 Brief the Class Reps

Small meeting or call with section representatives. Walk them through the tier structure, hand them the FAQ, and align on messaging so everyone says the same thing. Reps become the front line — they answer questions at their level so the committee isn't overwhelmed.

Step 4 Release Contribution Framework to the Batch

Now it goes out through the proper channel: Core → Class Reps → Batchmates. Reps are briefed, FAQ exists, and the committee is ready. No surprises.

Step 5 Flip the New Profile Live

Batchmates now see their full profile with contribution tier selection, payment upload, and progress tracking. The system is ready because the support structure is already in place.

Answering the Key Questions

These are the answers to the most common questions that will come up. This section will feed directly into the FAQ for class reps.

Can they change their tier?

Yes — upgrade or downgrade anytime. Just like RSVP. The new profile page handles this, and changes reflect on the admin side (Master List) automatically. No one is locked into a single choice.

It's "where you're starting, not where you're stuck."
Are they locked in?

No. Life changes, finances change. Someone who starts at Silver can move to Gold later, or vice versa. The system tracks total contributions regardless of tier changes. Flexibility builds trust and removes the biggest objection people have: "what if I commit and something happens?"

What's the journey after choosing a tier?

The payment process is designed to be transparent and verifiable at every step.

1
Choose a tier Batchmate selects their contribution tier from their profile. They see the total amount and monthly breakdown.
2
See payment options Payment channels are displayed (bank transfer, e-wallet, etc.) with account details.
3
Make payment Batchmate pays through their preferred channel, outside the website.
4
Upload receipt(s) Back on the website, they upload a photo or screenshot of their receipt. They can upload multiple receipts at once. The site handles storage.
5
Receipt lands in Receipts Inbox Controllers see all incoming receipts in one place. They click "Add to Ledger" — a new ledger row is created with the name and receipt pre-filled. Controller manually enters all financial details (amount, date, reference no., payment type).
6
Verification happens later The ledger entry starts as "Pending." When the controller checks the passbook and confirms the amount, they mark it "OK." Master List updates, and the batchmate's profile shows payment progress and full receipt history.
Personal archive: Every receipt they upload stays on their profile — timestamped, with verification status. Monthly contributors can scroll back through their entire payment history. It's protection for both sides if there's ever a question about a payment.

Communication Flow

Streamlined so the committee isn't overwhelmed. Information flows in one direction, questions flow back up through the same channel.

Core Committee Decisions, messaging, content
↓
Class Reps Briefed with FAQ, aligned on talking points
↓
Batchmates Receive information, ask questions through reps

Class reps are the multiplier. They extend the committee's reach without spreading the core team thin. Brief them properly once, equip them with the FAQ, and they handle the rest at their section level.

Why This Order Matters

If we release tiers before class reps are briefed and the FAQ exists, the committee gets flooded with questions and it looks unorganized. This sequence means by the time anyone sees a peso sign, the support structure is already in place.

✓ Registration first gives us real data on who's engaged before we talk money.
✓ Internal alignment prevents mixed messages going out to the batch.
✓ FAQs drafted before the reps meeting means reps walk away equipped, not confused.
✓ Reps briefed before public release means questions get answered at the section level.
✓ The new profile goes live only when every support layer is ready.

Dear Committee,

This document is the internal companion to the Contribution & Funding Plan we're presenting to the batch. Where the public plan shows what we're building, this one shows how we get there: the projections, the pressure points, and the decisions we need to align on.

The contribution plan follows a deliberate arc: start with pride → show the target → show transparency → explain impact → offer tiers → show recognition → invite participation. Every design choice supports this, from the progress bar at the top to the "flexible monthly commitments" messaging at the bottom.

Instead of presenting a traditional budget breakdown, each tier shows the events and experiences that level of contribution helps make possible. It's the simplest way to answer "where does my money go?" without handing anyone a spreadsheet.

Our ₱2.1M target is achievable through the hybrid model: contributions as the foundation, fundraising as the multiplier. The projections here are honest. The pressure test below is a reminder that the floor works.

This is for the committee's review. Nothing here is final until we're aligned. If you see gaps, have concerns, or think something should be framed differently — speak up. Your insights will only make this stronger. Let's get this right before it goes out.

Proceed accordingly.
— Felie

Key insight: Engagement drives funding success more than tier structure itself. People contribute to causes they feel connected to. Transparency, recognition, and authentic communication are the real levers.
Builder Tiers
What the batch sees vs. what we know
The plan uses Builder language deliberately. Everyone who contributes is a "Builder." Tiers are named after structural roles — Cornerstone, Pillar, Anchor, Root — to reinforce that the batch is building this together. No one is "just a donor," and no one is ranked. A traditional Platinum/Gold/Silver model creates a visible hierarchy based on how much someone can afford. The Builder framing avoids that — every contribution is a structural part of the whole. A Root is not less than a Cornerstone. They serve different roles in the same foundation.

Public-Facing Copy Guidance The internal rationale for Builder framing is solid: it avoids hierarchy and turns giving into shared ownership. But the public plan should make this explicit in one line. Add this sentence to the public contribution plan, near the tier heading:

"Each tier represents a different way of supporting the same goal — no one is 'higher,' just contributing differently."

Keep it to one sentence. Confident, not defensive. Don't over-explain.
How it works on the website
  • Tier selection is built into each member's profile at thegoldenbatch2003.com
  • When a batchmate picks a tier and enters their pledge amount, the plan automatically breaks it down into monthly, weekly, and daily amounts over the remaining months
  • For overseas batchmates, the "View in your currency" dropdown converts everything into their local currency (USD, AED, SGD, JPY, etc.) using live exchange rates
  • The goal: remove the mental math barrier so people commit on the spot instead of saying "I'll think about it"
  • Once confirmed, their chosen tier and pledge amount updates on their profile and is automatically reflected on the backend in the master list for committee tracking — Felie will walk through this in a recorded video
  • Payment methods are built right into the plan — PNB bank deposit for local and SWIFT transfer for international. After paying, batchmates upload their receipt on their profile for committee verification

Committee Implication If contributor count drops below 80 or Cornerstone/Pillar uptake is slow, the committee will need to step up with direct, personal outreach and sponsor engagement. This is a shared effort — it works best when each member taps into their own network. A personal message from someone they know goes further than any campaign. The goal isn't to pressure anyone into a tier — it's to make sure every batchmate knows the plan exists, understands what we're building, and feels invited to be part of it.

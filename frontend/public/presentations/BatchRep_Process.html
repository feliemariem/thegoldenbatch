How the Batch Rep Selection Works

This document explains how Batch 2003 will select its representative to the USLS Alumni Association. The process is designed to give every registered graduate a voice while keeping individual responses confidential. The committee facilitates — the batch decides.

Round 1 · Now Open
Nomination & Temperature Check

Every registered graduate sees a prompt on their profile asking two things: their nomination choice, and their personal willingness to serve.

1
Willingness question

Before anything else, the graduate is asked if they'd personally be willing to serve as Batch Rep if nominated. This is separate from their nomination choice — answering yes doesn't mean they're running, it just means they're available if the batch wants them.

2
Confirm Bia or nominate someone else

The graduate either confirms Bianca Jison as the proposed representative, or nominates another batchmate by name. They may also add a short comment. They can update their response anytime before the deadline.

3
Deadline

The nomination window is open for 5 days, closing at 11:59 PM PHT. After the deadline, the page locks and no further responses are accepted.

Confidentiality: Individual responses are not visible to anyone. Willingness answers and nominee names are only visible to the committee when reviewing results — so the batch knows Bianca's confirmation count and aggregate stats only.
After the Deadline
Committee Reviews Results

The organizing committee reviews the results together before any announcement is made to the batch.

What the committee sees
Total responses, confirmation count for Bianca, all nominees with willingness status, registration status, city, and country
What the batch sees
Aggregate stats only — no individual names or responses are ever shown publicly
Key question
Did any willing nominee emerge with significant nominations? This determines whether Round 2 is needed.

Decision Point

Based on Round 1 results, one of two paths is taken.

Path A — No contest

No willing nominee emerged with significant nominations. Bianca is confirmed as Batch Rep. The committee proceeds to the alumni office agreement form.

Path B — Round 2

One or more willing nominees emerged. A second vote is opened to the full batch — all willing candidates including Bianca appear on equal footing.

Round 2 · If Needed
Batch Vote

If Round 2 is triggered, the same page opens in a new phase. All registered graduates cast a single vote within a 5-day window, closing at 11:59 PM PHT.

1
Candidates presented

All willing candidates are shown — Bianca plus any nominees who confirmed willingness. Each candidate is labeled clearly (organizing committee member, batch-nominated, etc.).

2
Graduate votes

Each registered graduate picks one candidate. Votes are confidential. Graduates can update their vote before the Round 2 deadline.

3
Batch's choice determined

Candidate with the most votes becomes the batch's choice. Ties are resolved by the committee. Results are announced to the full batch.

Final Step
Batch Consensus & Submission

The batch's choice is announced with a short window for objections. This is how consensus is achieved.

1
Announcement

The committee posts the result to the batch — the batch's choice, vote count, and a brief summary of the process. Shared on Messenger and the website.

2
Objection window

A short window is given for anyone to raise concerns. Silence equals consensus — if no one objects, the result stands as the batch's unanimous decision.

3
Submission to alumni office

The confirmed Batch Rep signs the agreement form and is submitted to the USLS Alumni Association as Batch 2003's official representative.

Why this process works: Every graduate had a voice. The result came from the batch — not the committee. That's what makes it legitimate.

Why This Approach

✓ The batch decides — the committee only facilitates the process.
✓ All responses are confidential — no one can be pressured based on their answer.
✓ Willingness is verified before any name is put forward — no one is nominated without their knowledge.
✓ The process scales — Round 2 only happens if it's needed, keeping things simple by default.
✓ Consensus by silence gives everyone a final chance to object before the result is made official.
✓ The alumni office receives a result that came from a transparent, documented process — not just a committee decision.

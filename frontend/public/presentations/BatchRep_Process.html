How the Batch Rep Selection Works

A transparent, batch-driven process to select Batch 2003's representative to the USLS Alumni Association. The committee facilitates. The batch decides.

Total process: 15 days. Round 1 (7 days) · Round 2 if needed (7 days) · Objection window (24 hours). At the end, the batch's choice is confirmed by consensus and submitted to the alumni office.
Round 1 · Now Open
Nomination & Temperature Check

Every registered graduate sees a prompt on their profile with two questions: willingness to serve, and their nomination choice.

1
Willingness question

Each grad answers privately if they'd be willing to serve if nominated. This is separate from their nomination choice. Answering yes does not mean they are running.

2
Confirm Bia or nominate someone else

Confirm Bianca Jison as the proposed rep, or nominate another batchmate by name with an optional comment. Responses can be updated anytime before the deadline.

3
Deadline

7-day window, closing at 11:59 PM PHT. Page locks after the deadline.

Confidentiality: All responses are anonymous. Willingness answers and nominee names are visible to the committee only. The batch sees the outcome, not the stats.
After the Deadline
Committee Reviews Results

The committee reviews results before any announcement is made to the batch.

What the committee sees
Total responses, confirmation count for Bianca, all nominees with willingness status, registration status, city, and country
Key question
Did any willing nominee emerge? This determines whether Round 2 is needed.
What the batch sees
The outcome only. No stats, no names, no individual responses shared publicly.

Decision Point

Based on Round 1 results, one of two paths is taken.

Path A · No contest

No willing nominee emerged. Bianca is confirmed as Batch Rep. A 24-hour objection window opens before submission to the alumni office agreement form.

Path B · Round 2

A willing nominee emerged. A second vote opens to the full batch. All willing candidates including Bianca appear on equal footing.

Round 2 · If Needed
Batch Vote

The same page opens in a new phase. All registered graduates cast a single vote within a 7-day window, closing at 11:59 PM PHT.

1
Candidates presented

All willing candidates are shown: Bianca plus any nominees who confirmed willingness. Each candidate is labeled clearly.

2
Graduate votes

Each registered grad picks one candidate. Votes are confidential and can be updated before the deadline.

3
Batch's choice determined

Most votes takes it. Ties resolved by the committee. Results announced to the full batch.

Final Step
Batch Consensus & Submission

The batch's choice is announced with a short window for objections.

1
Announcement

The committee posts the result on Messenger, the website, and the FB page with a brief summary.

2
Objection window

24-hour window for anyone to raise concerns. Silence equals consensus.

3
Submission to alumni office

The confirmed Batch Rep signs the agreement form and is submitted to the USLS Alumni Association.

Why this works: Every graduate had a voice. The result came from the batch, not the committee. That is what makes it legitimate.

Why This Approach

✓ The batch decides. The committee only facilitates.
✓ All responses are confidential. No one can be pressured based on their answer.
✓ Willingness is verified before any name is put forward.
✓ Round 2 only happens if needed. Simple by default.
✓ Consensus by silence gives everyone a final chance to object.
✓ The alumni office receives a result from a transparent, documented process.

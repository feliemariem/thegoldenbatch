How the Payment System Works

This document explains how payments flow through the system — from the batchmate's profile to the accounting ledger. The goal is transparency for the batchmate and control for the financial controllers. No payment data is auto-generated. Controllers enter all financial details manually. The system's job is to deliver receipts to the right place so no one is chasing screenshots.

Batchmate Side
What They Experience

This is what the batchmate sees and does from their profile page after choosing a contribution tier.

1
Choose a tier

From their profile, the batchmate selects their contribution tier (Platinum, Gold, Silver, or Flex). They can change this anytime — upgrade or downgrade. It's where they're starting, not where they're stuck.

2
See payment options

Payment channels are displayed (bank transfer, e-wallet, etc.) with the account details they need to send money to.

3
Make payment

The batchmate pays through their preferred channel on their own — outside the website. This step happens in their banking app, not on our site.

4
Upload receipt(s)

Back on their profile, they upload a photo or screenshot of their receipt. They can upload multiple receipts at once (e.g., 3 months of installments). The site handles storage.

5
See their payment history

Their profile shows every receipt they've uploaded — timestamped, with verification status (Pending or Verified). This becomes their personal archive. Monthly contributors can scroll back through their full history.

From the batchmate's perspective: choose tier, pay, upload receipt, done. Everything after that happens on the admin side. They just check back to see their progress.
Controller Side
What the Admin Sees

Financial controllers manage everything from the admin dashboard. Two key areas: the Receipts Inbox and the Accounting Ledger.

Receipts Inbox

A new tab in the admin dashboard. This is where all uploaded receipts from batchmates land — one central place.

What's shown
Batchmate name, date uploaded, receipt image thumbnail, status
Statuses
New (just uploaded, unprocessed) and Processed (added to ledger)
Multiple uploads
If a batchmate uploads 3 receipts, all 3 appear as separate entries in the inbox
Purpose
No more chasing receipts through group chats or DMs. Everything is in one place, organized.

The "Add to Ledger" Process

When the controller is ready to process a receipt, here's what happens:

1
Controller opens a receipt from the inbox

They see the full receipt image, who uploaded it, and when.

2
Clicks "Add to Ledger"

A new ledger row is created automatically with two things pre-filled: the batchmate's name and the receipt image. Everything else is blank.

3
Controller fills in the details

Manually enters: transaction date, deposit amount, description (e.g., "East West Transfer"), reference number, and payment type (Full / Installment). This is the same process as today.

4
Receipt status updates

In the Receipts Inbox, that receipt flips from "New" to "Processed." The controller knows it's been handled.

5
Verification happens later

The ledger entry starts as "Pending." When the controller checks the passbook and confirms the amount, they mark it "OK" — just like the current workflow.

The controller stays in full control. The system only delivers the receipt and the name. All financial data — amounts, dates, reference numbers — are entered manually by the controller, just like today. The only difference: no more hunting for receipt screenshots.

What Updates Where

Once a receipt is processed and the ledger entry is verified, here's what gets updated across the system.

✓ Accounting Ledger New row with all transaction details, receipt attached, verified status.
✓ Master List Batchmate's total contribution and payment status updated.
✓ Batchmate's Profile Payment progress updated. Receipt appears in their personal payment history with verified status.
✓ Receipts Inbox Receipt marked as "Processed" — controller knows it's done.

Why This Approach

✓ Controllers stay in full control of the ledger — no auto-generated financial data.
✓ Receipts are organized in one place instead of scattered across group chats and DMs.
✓ "Add to Ledger" saves time by pre-attaching the receipt and name — no re-uploading.
✓ Batchmates have a personal archive of every payment they've made, with verification status.
✓ Protection for both sides — if there's ever a question about a payment, the receipt is right there.
✓ Scales for installment payers — monthly contributors upload each receipt, building a full history over 33 months.
